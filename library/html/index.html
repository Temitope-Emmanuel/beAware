WAYS TO MAKE YOUR WORKSPACE

Accessible workplaces are win-win situations: employees feel valued and accommodated, which boosts productivity, raises morale, and results in a more successful business. Roughly 14% of the population is disabled, so making your workplace welcoming means you can tap into a talented pool of prospective employees. Further, accessibility is not nearly as expensive as many employers fear. Here are just a handful of ways workplaces can be physically, technologically, and culturally accessible. GO PAPERLESS

People living with disabilities, especially does with visual impairment, benefit from electronic communication.The advantage being that electronic documents are more flexible; with employer being able to adjust contrast,background color and font size and also open the use of using screen reader plus, you get to save trees!

A LOVING ENVIRONMENT

Creating an inclusive workspace is more than specialized software and physically accessible environment. Everyone in the workspace need to be aware of behaviour and attitude that may cause harm, whether intentional or unintentional, it is also vital to recognize emotional barrier that may inhibit disabled employees from being productive as other coworkers.It is advised that other employees to use appropiate language and discourage them from devaluing their colleague or assume that people with disabilities cannot contribute equally

MAKE USE OF ASSISTIVE TECHNOLOGY

The use of adaptive, assistive and rehabilitation devices is appreciated, such devices promotes independences and decreases the need for support allowing them to care for themselves and fully carry out their duty.

BE SPACIOUS

No one wants a stuffy environment,make the hallways,and office cubics as spacious as possible, Spacious and fully equipped office and collaborative meeting room make teamwork and meeting that are much more enjoyable, comfortable and productive.
